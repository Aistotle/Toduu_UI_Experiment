01 / Scale

Size is a shout.

Larger type commands attention before a single word is read. It establishes hierarchy and emotional tone.

02 / Weight

light light normal bold black

Weight modulates voice—from whisper to roar—without changing words.

03 / Rhythm & Line Length

Lines too long tire the eye. Too short break the rhythm. Around 45–75 characters per line keeps readers in flow.

Measure controls pacing, like breath in poetry.
